Max Temperature




Compared to the other metrics plotted against latitude, the maximum temperature of each 771 randomly selected cities has a negative parabolic distribution. Simply, as the latitude increases from the range -80 to +80 degrees, the max temperature increases, peaks, and then begins to decreases. This demonstrates that there is an increase in max temperature as the city is closer to the equator (0 degree latitude).

However, the negative parabolic distribution is not clearly defined. The plot displays a cluster of cities within the latitude range from ~45% to ~75% perturbing out of the negative parabolic curve. Furthermore, it appears more cities with positive latitudes were collected, despite utilizing the random module to generate coordinate pairs well-distributed throughout the world. Referring back to the jupyter notebook on the landing page, a plot displaying the distribution of 2000 randomly generated coordinate pairs is available. From the 2000 randomly generated coordinate pairs, only 771 cities were identified near the coordinate pairs.
